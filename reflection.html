Reflection

MATH 490

This course was one of the core factors in my decision to pursue a minor in mathematics. I learned about control of quantum systems as an extension to my Differential Equations course, specifically how they applied to fully actuated systems and their optimal solutions. For example, take the system of a car trying to parallel park; the controls would be the steering wheel, gas, gear, and brake and the solution is the car being in the parking spot. The task would be to determine an optimal series of inputs to the controls in order to reach the solution based on, for example, time. This work greatly improved my appreciation for mathematics and has fueled my interest in personal projects concerning the discrete application of continuous models, such as very simple physics engines.


LING 119

I learned a lot about grammar and was able to apply my knowledge to the field of computer languages to some degree after completing this Linguistics course. It was interesting to draw parallels between the format of computer and human languages in how meaning is conveyed.

Cumulative Reflection

Iowa State University has, through group based classes such as 309 and 329, taught me many valuable lessons in working in a group. Not the least of which is how to balance working with and managing peers in the case that there is a large disparity in the technical knowledge between group members. This includes identifying strengths and delegating tasks to group members based on them. It also includes being able to take people of differing skill sets and break a problem into assignable portions so that individuals can come together as a group to work towards a common goal.

The open-ended and free nature of the project based classes I took have granted me the ability to create projects and solve them from an engineering standpoint multiple times over. I was able to select a real world problem I wanted to solve, translate that into a project with specifications, and then break it down further into programmable parts that were abel to be delivered.

In a global context, my ability to formulate engineering solutions have been honed so that I can contribute to the advancement of humanity and improve the lives of people for whom I create software. I am able to save people's time by automating tasks and save resources by optimizing solutions.

My work in groups at ISU has greatly improved my ability to take that experience to the real world and work in groups both professionally and personally. I have learned the importance of quickly identifying the strengths that each member brings to the team and how to harness those in order to create an environment where everyone can feel as though they are a contributing member fo the team, which increases output and makes everyone involved happier.

In trying to come up with problems to solve for my project based classes, I have been able to identify pain points in everyday life and use the issues to make ideas for interesting projects or products that could be created to solve them. For example, I hate doing jigsaw puzzles, so I thought that it would be helpful to create an image processing app that could help identify where pieces should go so that they can be solved quickly and with more efficiency.

I am better able to understand my professional and ethical responsibilities after my time at ISU since I have learned how to abide my a standard code of ethics such as those set forth by IEEE. I am able to recognize why these statutes exist and how abiding by them is a benefit for me and everyone who I associate with.

In my classes, I was always able to use the expertize of the faculty in order to complete the required work. I could depend on the resources that were afforded to me by the labs and library in order to use software that was helpful to me in the completion and compilation of programs that I was tasked with during my time here.

My Undergraduate Research experience and experience at ISU sponsored career fairs helped shape my view of how to approach real world problems. Through my research I was able to take a more academic view of problems and behaviors that I was experiencing in my life and understand the more theoretical basis behind them. From my experience in the career fair I was able to better see who I was in the eyes of the professional world and be more aware of my self in that context.

I have pursued multiple internship experiences in order to improve my ability to apply my academic knowledge to a professional rather than an academic environment. In these situations, functionality is imposed upon the team rather than supplied for the express purpose of learning as in the more academic contexts. I have also started many different personal projects in order to fill in the gaps in what my academic experience and internships are able to provide.

The main thing that I would change if I were to complete my undergraduate work again would to be more active in the student organizations and depend on my academic peers in order to use what they hae learned in order to have a more balanced and full understanding of my courses.
